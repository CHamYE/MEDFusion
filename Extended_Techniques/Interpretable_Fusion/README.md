# Interpretability & Reliability Optimization for Multi-Modality Medical Image Fusion
## 📌 Overview
Interpretability and reliability are the core requirements for the clinical implementation of multi-modal medical image fusion (MMIF) technology. Existing deep learning-based fusion methods are mostly "black box" models, which cannot quantify the contribution of each modality to the fusion result, nor explain the decision-making basis of the model, which limits the trust of clinicians in the fusion results. In addition, the reliability of fusion results under different modal qualities, noise, and pathological changes is also a key clinical concern.

This section summarizes the representative methods for interpretability and reliability optimization of MMIF, which mainly solve the problem through evidence theory, uncertainty modeling, modality reliability learning, pixel-level attribution visualization, and other technical paths.

| Evidence modeling for reliability learning and interpretable decision-making under multi-modality medical image segmentation | Computerized Medical Imaging and Graphics (CMIG), 2024 | Proposes CDE-Net, which introduces Dempster-Shafer evidence theory (DST) into multi-modal medical image segmentation for the first time. It designs an evidence decomposition and mapping module (EDMM) to convert semantic features into mass functions, a context discount fusion layer (CDFL) to learn category-level reliability coefficients of each modality, and a reliability-weighted pixel attribution method (RW-PAM) to realize pixel-level interpretable visualization. | [Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0895611124000995) | Not publicly available |
| Multi-Modality Uncertainty for Medical Image Segmentation | 5th International Conference on Neural Networks, Information and Communication Engineering (NNICE), 2025 | Mathematically proves that the multi-modal uncertainty is less than or equal to the maximum uncertainty of single modality in late fusion, theoretically explaining the performance advantage of multi-modal fusion. It proposes an uncertainty-driven feature fusion strategy, which estimates feature uncertainty via MC-Dropout, converts it into confidence weights, and improves the reliability of fusion results. | [Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11064419) | Not publicly available |
| MAINet: Modality-Aware Interaction Network for Medical Image Fusion | ACM Transactions on Multimedia Computing, Communications, and Applications (TMCCA), 2025 | Proposes MAINet with a cascaded modality interaction (CMI) module, which can adaptively weight the features of different modalities according to the information richness of each modality, and realizes interpretable modality-aware fusion. The dual graph learning (DGL) module can visualize the contribution of different regions and channels to the fusion result. | [Link](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3731247) | Not publicly available |

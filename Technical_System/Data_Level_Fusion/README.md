# Data-level Multi-Modality Medical Image Fusion
## 📌 Overview
Data-level fusion (also known as pixel-level/voxel-level fusion) is the most low-level fusion paradigm in multi-modality medical image fusion (MMIF). It directly integrates the pixel/voxel values of pre-registered multi-modal medical images at the front end of data acquisition.

The core goal of data-level fusion is to **completely retain the original detail information of each modality** (e.g., bone structure from CT, soft tissue texture from MRI, metabolic information from PET/SPECT), and generate a single high-quality fused image that contains complementary information from all source modalities. It is the core technology for clinical visual diagnosis and post-processing of multi-modal image registration, and also an important pre-foundation for feature-level and decision-level fusion.

### 1. Traditional Non-Deep Learning Methods
| Full Paper Title | Publication Venue & Year | Core Contributions | Paper Link | Open-Source Code |
|-------------------|---------------------------|--------------------|------------|------------------|
| Multi-Modality Medical Image Fusion Using SWT & Speckle Noise Reduction with Bidirectional Exact Pattern Matching Algorithm | Disruptive Technologies for Society 5.0, 2021 | Proposes a non-deep learning data-level fusion method combining stationary wavelet transformation (SWT) and speckle noise reduction (SNR) technology, with a bidirectional exact pattern matching algorithm to optimize detail retention for medical images. | [Link](https://www.taylorfrancis.com/chapters/edit/10.1201/9781003154686-20/multi-modality-medical-image-fusion-using-swt-speckle-noise-reduction-bidirectional-exact-pattern-matching-algorithm-kapil-joshi-minakshi-memoria-laxman-singh-parag-verma-archana-barthwal) | Not publicly available |
| Medical Image Fusion via Convolutional Sparsity Based Morphological Component Analysis | IEEE Signal Processing Letters, 2019 | Proposes a CS-MCA model for medical image fusion, which obtains the CSR of cartoon and texture components via pre-learned dictionaries, merges sparse coefficients, and reconstructs the fused image. It outperforms traditional SR-based fusion methods in both visual perception and objective evaluation. | [Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/8627371) | Not publicly available |
| Multimodal medical image fusion based on IHS and PCA | Procedia Engineering, 2010 | Combines IHS (Intensity-Hue-Saturation) transform and PCA (Principal Component Analysis) for multi-modal medical image fusion, solving the spectral distortion problem of traditional IHS fusion, and is suitable for PET-MRI/CT-MRI fusion. | [Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1877705810004501) | Not publicly available |
| SWT and PCA image fusion methods for multi-modal imagery | Multimedia Tools and Applications, 2019 | Systematically compares and optimizes SWT (Stationary Wavelet Transform) and PCA based fusion methods for multi-modal medical imagery, providing a practical baseline for pixel-level fusion of medical images with different resolution and contrast. | [Link](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11042-018-6098-0) | Not publicly available |
| Integrated volume visualization of functional image data and anatomical surfaces using normal fusion | Human Brain Mapping, 2001 | Proposes a normal fusion method for 3D volume visualization of functional (PET/SPECT) and anatomical (MRI) medical images, realizing voxel-level fusion for 3D medical volume data, which is the early classic work of 3D data-level medical image fusion. | [Link](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/hbm.1038) | Not publicly available |
